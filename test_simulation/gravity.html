Gravity

Gravity is a fundamental force of nature that attracts all objects with mass towards each other.

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

$$ F = G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2} $$

  • F = Gravitational force
  • G = Gravitational constant (6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N⋅m²/kg²)
  • m₁, m₂ = Masses of objects
  • r = Distance between centers of masses

Gravitational Field

The gravitational field strength (g) at Earth's surface:

$$ g = \frac{GM}{R^2} \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2 $$

  • Varies with altitude: $$ g(h) = g_0\left(\frac{R}{R+h}\right)^2 $$
  • Varies with latitude
  • Depends on local mass distribution

Gravitational Potential Energy

$$ PE = mgh $$

$$ PE = -\frac{GMm}{r} \text{ (general form)} $$

  • m = mass of object
  • g = gravitational field strength
  • h = height above reference point
Escape Velocity

$$ v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}} $$

Earth's escape velocity: 11.2 km/s

Review Questions

  1. Question: Calculate the gravitational force between two objects with masses 1000 kg and 2000 kg, separated by 10 meters.

    Answer: F = G(m₁m₂/r²) = (6.674×10⁻¹¹)(1000×2000)/(10²) = 1.335×10⁻⁶ N

  2. Question: Why do astronauts appear weightless in orbit despite being within Earth's gravitational field?

    Answer: They are in constant free fall around Earth, but their horizontal velocity prevents them from falling to Earth. Both the astronauts and their spacecraft are falling at the same rate, creating the appearance of weightlessness.

  3. Question: What is the escape velocity from a planet with twice Earth's mass and the same radius as Earth?

    Answer: Since v_e ∝ √M, doubling the mass increases escape velocity by √2. Therefore, new v_e = 11.2×√2 = 15.84 km/s

  4. Question: How does the gravitational force change when you double the distance between two objects?

    Answer: Since F ∝ 1/r², doubling the distance reduces the force to ¼ of its original value.
