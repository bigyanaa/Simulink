Understanding Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole, allowing us to work with quantities that aren't whole numbers.

Basic Concepts

Parts of a Fraction

$$ \frac{Numerator}{Denominator} $$

  • Numerator: Number of parts taken
  • Denominator: Total number of equal parts
Types of Fractions

Proper Fraction: $$ \frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4} $$ (numerator < denominator)

Improper Fraction: $$ \frac{5}{3}, \frac{7}{4} $$ (numerator > denominator)

Mixed Number: $$ 1\frac{1}{2}, 2\frac{3}{4} $$ (whole number + proper fraction)

Operations with Fractions

Addition and Subtraction

With same denominators:

$$ \frac{a}{c} \pm \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a \pm b}{c} $$

With different denominators:

$$ \frac{a}{b} \pm \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad \pm bc}{bd} $$

Multiplication and Division

Multiplication:

$$ \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ac}{bd} $$

Division:

$$ \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{ad}{bc} $$

Simplifying Fractions

Steps to Simplify
  1. Find the GCD (Greatest Common Divisor) of numerator and denominator
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by the GCD

Example:

$$ \frac{24}{36} = \frac{24 \div 12}{36 \div 12} = \frac{2}{3} $$

Common Applications

Field Applications Examples
Cooking Recipe measurements ½ cup sugar, ¾ teaspoon salt
Construction Measurements and scaling ⅝ inch plywood, ¼ inch drill bit
Finance Percentages and ratios ¾ of total budget, ½ yearly interest

Review Questions

  1. Question: Simplify the fraction $\frac{48}{64}$

    Answer: $\frac{48 \div 16}{64 \div 16} = \frac{3}{4}$

  2. Question: Add $\frac{3}{8}$ and $\frac{5}{8}$

    Answer: $\frac{3}{8} + \frac{5}{8} = \frac{8}{8} = 1$

  3. Question: Convert $2\frac{3}{4}$ to improper fraction

    Answer: $\frac{(2 \times 4) + 3}{4} = \frac{11}{4}$
