Potential Energy

Potential energy is the stored energy possessed by an object due to its position, configuration, or state. This energy has the potential to be converted into kinetic energy.

Types of Potential Energy

Gravitational

$$ PE_g = mgh $$

  • m = mass
  • g = gravitational field (9.81 m/s²)
  • h = height

Elastic

$$ PE_e = \frac{1}{2}kx^2 $$

  • k = spring constant
  • x = displacement

Electric

$$ PE_e = k\frac{q_1q_2}{r} $$

  • k = Coulomb's constant
  • q = charges
  • r = distance

Conservation of Energy

The total mechanical energy in a closed system remains constant:

$$ E_{total} = KE + PE = constant $$

$$ \frac{1}{2}mv^2 + mgh = constant $$

Applications

Engineering Applications

  • Hydroelectric power generation
  • Spring-loaded devices
  • Roller coasters
  • Energy storage systems

Natural Examples

  • Water cycle
  • Chemical bonds
  • Planetary orbits
  • Stretched rubber bands

Review Questions

  1. Question: A 2 kg object is lifted to a height of 5 meters. Calculate its gravitational potential energy. (g = 9.81 m/s²)

    Answer: $$\begin{align*} PE &= mgh \\ &= 2\text{ kg} \times 9.81\text{ m/s}^2 \times 5\text{ m} \\ &= 98.1\text{ J} \end{align*}$$

  2. Question: A spring has a spring constant of 100 N/m. How much potential energy is stored when it is compressed by 0.2 meters?

    Answer: $$\begin{align*} PE &= \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \times 100\text{ N/m} \times (0.2\text{ m})^2 \\ &= 2\text{ J} \end{align*}$$

  3. Question: Why does stretching a rubber band make it feel warmer?

    Answer: Some of the potential energy stored in the stretched rubber band $$\begin{align*} PE &= \frac{1}{2}kx^2 \end{align*}$$ is converted to thermal energy due to internal friction.

  4. Question: If a ball is thrown straight up, at what point is its potential energy maximum?

    Answer: At the highest point of its trajectory, where velocity is zero and thus kinetic energy $$\begin{align*} KE &= \frac{1}{2}mv^2 = 0 \end{align*}$$
